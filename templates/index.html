    What is Climate Change?

    A change which is attributed directly or indirectly to human activity that alters the composition of the global atmosphere and which is in addition to natural climate variability observed over comparable time periods.

    Useful Links

    IPCC

    IPCC is an intergovernmental body of the UN that is dedicated to providing the world with the risk and impacts of human-induced climate change and possible response options.

    U.N. Climate Action

    The UN has made environmental issues a prominent part of its agenda. Here you can find updated news articles, reports, campaigns, solutions, and much more.

    Center for Climate and Energy Solutions

    The Center for Climate and Energy Solutions is an environmental nonprofit organization based in Arlington, Virginia.

    National Centers for Environmental Information

    The Nation’s leading authority for environmental data, and manage one of the largest archives of atmospheric, coastal, geophysical, and oceanic research in the world.

    USGCRP

    A federal program mandated by Congress to coordinate federal research and investments in understanding the forces shaping the global environment, both human and natural, and their impacts on society.

    NCAR

    NCAR was established by the National Science Foundation in 1960 to provide the university community with world-class facilities and services that were beyond the reach of any individual institution.
